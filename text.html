The power of music through the decades

What is music?

define Music The official definition of music states that music is a vocal or instrumental sound (or both) combined in such a way as to produce beauty of form, harmony, and expression of emotion. The word music comes from the Greek word (mousike), which means "(art) of the Muses". Music is a form of art; an expression of emotions through harmonic frequencies. The definition of music can be different depending on a person's perspective or view on it. For each individual, music may represent something different, which makes the actual definition of music go way deeper than just words. A written difinition is one thing but the experience of actually listening to music and feeling the emotion and frequency of the art of music is where the true definition resides to each individual.

What decades will we talk about?

Music has changed dramatically through the years. Throughout the years, musical styles have reflected the society of the time and have evolved with changes in the world. Not only does music change with society but it changes with technological advances as well. As technology changes it can allow new styles to emerge and new ways for people to listen. Music trends change constantly day by day, so to understand where all of this started and where some music genres were at their peak, I have created a list of what genres of music define each decade.

  1. the 1920s was dominated by jazz, blues and the traveling dance bands
  2. the 1930s had popular genres like Swing, Jazz, Country, Traditional Pop, Crooners, and Big Band
  3. the 1940s consisted mainly of jazz, big band, and swing. It reflected the pain of World War II while also trying to remain upbeat and looking towards a positive future full of possibilities.
  4. the 1950s was filled with traditional Pop, Rock 'n' Roll, Rhythm & Blues, Country, Jazz, Blues, Calypso, Vocal Jazz. It reflected the beginnings of major social changes in the world and in the US, especially.
  5. the 1960s consisted of Folk, Surf Rock, Psychedelic Rock, Blues-Rock, Progressive Rock, Garage Rock, Pop, Soul, R&B, Country, British Invasion, Latin Rock, Protest/Music with a Message
  6. the 1970s had Disco/Dance, Progressive Rock, Punk, New Wave, Funk, Soul, Glam Rock, Soft Rock, Singer-Songwriter, Folk, Souther Rock/Country Rock, Country-Pop, Power Pop
  7. the 1980s had New Wave, Synth-Pop, Hair Metal, Hip-Hop, Gothic Rock, Heavy Metal, Pop, Alternative Rock, Hardcore Punk, Contemporary R&B, Country
  8. the 1990s was known for the grunge era with Alternative/College Rock, Technotronic, Hip-Hop, Gangster Rap, Bubblegum Pop, Boy Bands/Girl Groups, Pop-Punk, Metal, Ska, Contemporary R&B, Country-Pop, Britpop, New Jack Swing, Singer-Songwriter, Hard Rock, Happy Rock
  9. the 2000s had Dance-Pop, Indie Rock, Emo, Pop-Punk, Contemporary R&B, Hip-Hop, Reggaeton, Electronica, Hard Rock, Alternative Metal, New Wave Revival, Teen Pop, Boy Bands, Internet Stars, Disney Artists, Adult Contemporary, Country, Country-Pop, British Soul, Latin Pop
